Where are people fleeing from?

From the fire of Middle East

There are more refugees from Syria than any other country in the world, according to the UNHCR. The majority of the 4.2m people fleeing the four years of civil war have found refuge in surrounding states such as Turkey. Despite a new record being broken, Europe’s share of asylum seekers compared with these countries has been relatively small. Ongoing conflicts and political instability in both Iraq and Afghanistan means that many of their nationals are also trying to make it into the EU. There have been 294,280 asylum applications from Syria, Iraq and Afghanistan so far this year, with more than a quarter going to Germany.

From the ashes of Yugoslavia

Eurostat has recorded 134,125 applications from citizens of Kosovo, Albania and Serbia so far in 2015, which is a 56.4% increase on the total for 2014. These three countries were involved in the instability around the break-up of former Yugoslavia, which was the main driver of asylum applications and refugees in the previous record year of 1992. But most asylum applications from these countries today are likely to be rejected as they are all now deemed “safe" by the EU.

A comparison between 1992 and 2015

Germany

Germany has received more asylum seekers so far in 2015 than any other EU country with 245,615 applications lodged by the end of August. Angela Merkel has spoken on several occasions about the need for other EU member states to share responsibility in welcoming refugees. Germany has welomed 51,360 asylum applications from Syrians up till the end of August.

France

France joined with Germany in a successful call for a mandatory redistribution of Syrian, Afghan and Eritrean refugees across EU countries over the next few years. Citizens of those three countries combined have contributed just 2,835 to France's total asylum applications this year. Kosovans have submitted the most (3,230).

In 1991, Italy lodged 18,700 applications for asylum from Albanians, more than seven times the grand total number of applications the country received in 1992. This year over 100,000 people have crossed the Mediterranean attempting to reach Italy's shores, according to the International Organisation for Migration, with thousands dying along the way.

Methodological note
There are now 28 EU member states, while there were only 15 in 1992. The data from 1992 is not as complete as today's figures, which makes an accurate state-by-state difficult. In several cases only an estimate is possible. Eurostat figures are subject to rounding so sums may not add-up.
