Where are people fleeing from?

From the fire of Middle East

There are more refugees from Syria than any other country in the world, according to the UNHCR. Nearly four million have left during the four years of civil war. Half of these people are currently in Turkey, so the numbers that have entered Europe remain relatively small by comparison. A majority that do travel to Europe go to Germany, which has received more Syrian refugees than any other EU member this year, and to Sweden, which has welcomed the most per capita.

From the ashes of Yugoslavia

Kosovo was one of the flash points in 1992 as tensions began to rise in the crumbling state of former Yugoslavia, which it used to be a part of. Kosovo declared independence from Serbia in 2008 but several countries do not recognise its sovereignty. Most Kosovans like others from the Balkan states who seek asylum today are likely to have their applications refused.
