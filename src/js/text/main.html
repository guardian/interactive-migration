How migration to Europe has changed in the last two decades

A record number of people has sought asylum in the European Union this year.
With four months of data still to come, this year's figures have already passed the previous peak reached in 1992 when 672,835 asylum applications were filed across the EU.
Today hundreds of thousands of people are fleeing conflicts in Syria, Afghanistan and Iraq, and poverty and dictatorships elsewhere. In 1992, hundreds of thousands were fleeing a war in former Yugoslavia and unrest in its neighbouring countries.
Here's a look at how yesterday's figures compare to this year's.
